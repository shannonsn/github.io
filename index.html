Historic Places in the V&A Waterfront

Clock Tower

  • The V&A Waterfront Clock Tower is situated in Table Bay Harbour
  • The clock tower was used to check tide levels back than
  • The clock tower is still in use today

African Trading Port

  • The African Trading Port has the enviable reputation of being one of the 'must stop'
    spots for African arts, crafts and furniture at Cape Town’s famous V&A Waterfront.
  • It offers three floors of art and craft suitable for collections, home decor and personal use.
  • You will find one of the widest ranges of large, collectable items such as sculpted giraffes that stand six feet high,
    chairs and tables made from indigenous hardwoods, stone carvings that weigh several kilograms,
    beautiful hand-crafted pottery or delicate ostrich eggs

The Swing Bridge

  • The bridge swings over the link between the Alfred Basin and the Victoria Basin in the Victoria & Alfred Waterfront.
    It was designed and constructed in 1997.

  • ​Situated at the foot of Table Mountain in South Africa’s iconic Waterfront, lies the V&A Marina.
    Within a protected harbour environment, the V&A Marina offers the cruising visitor a berth and a host of services and facilities in the greater V&A Waterfront.
    The V&A Marina is accessed from Granger Bay through the Victoria and the Alfred Basins under the Swing Bridge.
